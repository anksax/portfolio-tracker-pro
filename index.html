Service Requests & Instructions

Systematic Withdrawal Plan (SWP)

Purpose: Generate regular income by withdrawing a fixed amount periodically from your mutual funds.

Steps: 1. Log in to AMC portal or CAMS. 2. Select Folio. 3. Go to Transactions > SWP. 4. Set frequency (monthly/quarterly) and amount. 5. Confirm via OTP.

Redemption Issues & Tracking

Purpose: Selling your mutual fund units or stocks to transfer money to your bank.

Steps: Ensure KYC is updated. Equity redemptions take T+2 days. Liquid funds take T+1 day. If delayed, check bank mandate rejection or contact CAMS/KFintech support.

Systematic Transfer Plan (STP)

Purpose: Move money from a low-risk fund (Liquid) to a high-risk fund (Equity) systematically to average out market volatility.

Steps: Both funds must be in the same AMC. Select Liquid Fund -> Transaction -> STP -> Target Equity Fund -> Set frequency and amount.

Key Investment Statistical Indicators

Alpha (α)

Definition: Measures a portfolio's return against a benchmark index (like Nifty 50). A positive Alpha means the investment outperformed the market.

Tip: Look for actively managed Mutual Funds with an Alpha > 1.0 consistently over 3-5 years.

Beta (β)

Definition: Measures volatility or systemic risk. A Beta of 1.0 means it moves exactly with the market. Beta < 1 is less volatile, Beta > 1 is more volatile.

Tip: If you are risk-averse, build a portfolio with a Beta < 0.85 (add large caps and debt).

Sharpe Ratio

Definition: Measures risk-adjusted return. It tells you how much excess return you are receiving for the extra volatility you endure.

Tip: A Sharpe Ratio > 1 is good, > 2 is very good, and > 3 is excellent. Always compare Sharpe ratios of funds within the same category.

XIRR (Extended Internal Rate of Return)

Definition: The actual annualized return for multiple cash flows (like SIPs) occurring at irregular intervals.

Tip: For mutual fund SIPs, always look at XIRR, not absolute return, to know your true compounding rate.
